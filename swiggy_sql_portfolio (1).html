Core Queries

SQL Analytics Library

🧹
Duplicate Detection & Removal Cleaning 
-- Find duplicates
SELECT State, City, Restaurant_Name,
       Dish_Name, Price, Rating,
       COUNT(*) AS CNT
FROM swiggy_data
GROUP BY State, City, Restaurant_Name,
         Dish_Name, Price, Rating
HAVING COUNT(*) > 1;

-- Delete via ROW_NUMBER()
DELETE S1 FROM swiggy_data S1
JOIN (
  SELECT Id FROM (
    SELECT Id,
      ROW_NUMBER() OVER (
        PARTITION BY State, City,
        Restaurant_Name, Dish_Name
        ORDER BY (SELECT NULL)
      ) AS Rn
    FROM swiggy_data) t
  WHERE Rn > 1) S2
ON S1.Id = S2.Id;

Revenue KPIs KPI 
-- Total Revenue in INR Millions
SELECT
  CONCAT(
    FORMAT(SUM(
      CAST(Price_INR AS DECIMAL(10,2))
    ) / 1000000, 2),
    ' INR Millions'
  ) AS Total_Revenue
FROM fact_swiggy;

-- Average Dish Price
SELECT
  CONCAT(
    FORMAT(AVG(
      CAST(Price_INR AS DECIMAL(10,2))
    ), 2),
    ' INR'
  ) AS Avg_Dish_Price
FROM fact_swiggy;
◆ Total Revenue: 53.00 INR Million across all orders
📅
Monthly Order Trends Time Series 
SELECT
  d.Year, d.Month, d.Month_name,
  COUNT(*) AS Total_Orders,
  SUM(f.Price_INR) AS Monthly_Revenue
FROM fact_swiggy f
JOIN dim_date d
  ON f.date_id = d.date_id
GROUP BY
  d.Year, d.Month, d.Month_name
ORDER BY d.Year, d.Month;
◆ Peak orders in Jan, May & Aug. Higher demand at start of year.
🏙️
Top 10 Cities by Orders Location 
SELECT
  dl.City AS City,
  COUNT(*) AS Highest_Orders,
  SUM(fs.Price_INR) AS Revenue
FROM fact_swiggy fs
JOIN dim_location dl
  ON fs.location_id = dl.location_id
GROUP BY dl.City
ORDER BY COUNT(*) DESC
LIMIT 10;
📊
Quarterly Revenue Trends Time Series 
SELECT
  d.Year, d.Quarter,
  COUNT(*) AS Quarterly_Orders,
  SUM(f.Price_INR) AS Quarterly_Revenue
FROM fact_swiggy f
JOIN dim_date d
  ON f.date_id = d.date_id
GROUP BY d.Year, d.Quarter
ORDER BY
  SUM(f.Price_INR) DESC;
◆ Q2 peaks in both orders and revenue across all years.
🍛
Cuisine Orders + Avg Rating Food 
SELECT
  c.Category AS Category,
  COUNT(*) AS Total_Orders,
  ROUND(AVG(fs.Rating), 2)
    AS Avg_Rating
FROM fact_swiggy fs
JOIN dim_category c
  ON fs.category_id = c.category_id
GROUP BY c.Category
ORDER BY Total_Orders DESC;
💲
Order Volume by Price Range Pricing 
SELECT
  CASE
    WHEN Price_INR < 100
      THEN 'Under 100'
    WHEN Price_INR BETWEEN
      100 AND 199
      THEN '100–199'
    WHEN Price_INR BETWEEN
      200 AND 299
      THEN '200–299'
    WHEN Price_INR BETWEEN
      300 AND 499
      THEN '300–499'
    ELSE '500+'
  END AS Price_range,
  COUNT(*) AS Total_orders
FROM fact_swiggy
GROUP BY Price_range
ORDER BY Total_orders DESC;
📆
Revenue by Day of Week Behavioral 
SELECT
  DAYNAME(d.Full_date)
    AS Week_Day,
  DATE_FORMAT(d.Full_date,'%w')
    AS Week_No,
  SUM(f.Price_INR)
    AS Weekly_Revenue
FROM fact_swiggy f
JOIN dim_date d
  ON f.date_id = d.date_id
GROUP BY
  DAYNAME(d.Full_date),
  DATE_FORMAT(d.Full_date,'%w')
ORDER BY Weekly_Revenue DESC;
◆ Weekends dominate — highest orders and revenue observed on Sat & Sun.

Seasonal Demand Spikes

Orders peak in January, May, and August — suggesting seasonal promotions or festivals drive usage at year-start.

🗓️

Q2 is the Revenue Leader

Second quarter consistently outperforms others in both order volume and total revenue across all years in the dataset.

🏙️

City-Level Concentration

Top 10 cities drive a disproportionate share of orders and revenue, reflecting urban density and delivery network strength.

🍕

Category × Rating Correlation

Analysis of cuisine categories reveals which food types earn both high order counts and high customer ratings simultaneously.

📅

Weekend Revenue Uplift

Saturday and Sunday generate significantly higher order volumes and revenue compared to weekdays — a clear behavioral pattern.

💲

Mid-Range Pricing Dominates

The ₹100–299 price band captures the majority of orders, pointing to a value-conscious customer base on the platform.
